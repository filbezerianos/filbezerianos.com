LLMs and Text-Generation AI Solutions Popularity

TO-WRITE

Jul 21, 2024 - 3 minute read

LLMs have revolutionised text-based AI solutions, with significant impact across industries. While AI text-generation solutions can deliver multiple benefits across multiple workflows and can be applied to different solution types, it is not always the best choice.

The popularity of General-Purpose Models

Several organisations are increasingly looking to adopt AI solutions to improve efficiency and innovation, from customer service and content creation to data analysis. In many cases what is observed is that organisations start from the technology, e.g. usage of general-purpose LLMs for text-generation, and look for relevant use cases within their industry and organisation to apply the technology. This approach poses several risks, as organisation may miss valid and very valuable use cases through leveraging other types of models.

That is understood up to a certain point as the news are dominated from announcements around these LLMs and their use. While these models are powerful, in many cases general-purpose tools, they are not always the best option for every task.

Task-Specific Models

Many tasks benefit more from specialised, often smaller, models, such-as for image-processing, task-specific questioning-answering etc. These models are optimised for particular types of data and tasks, allowing for more accurate, efficient and relevant outcomes in certain applications.

In addition, in some cases the output of the general-purpose text-generation models will not return the output in a format easy for post-processing. While there are some techniques, like prompt engineering, to format the output to specific structure, this is not guaranteed due to the probabilistic nature of these models. Thus, in many cases the output format of these specialised models is more appropriate for further processing allowing more accurate results.

Maintaining familiar workflows for the user

As in our previous article [link here], when integrating AI into a product or workflow, user experience matters. In our reference solution for this series, our goal was to maintain a familiar workflow for the user, which is augmented with AI. We did not want to rely on the user to prompt the model in a specific way to get the most accurate results, and we wanted to have a standardised model to be used across the solution, independently from user input.

While a chatbot could be used in our use case, we opted-in for a “visual questioning-answering” model.

Image-processing and questioning-answering

In our reference solution for this series (an MVP can be accessed here) the challenge faced was around how we can identify specific characteristics of property photos. While a general-purpose model could identify some of these characteristics, and in some cases with better accuracy, it has been proven very challenging to control the output format across all the images input (we have processed more than 300k images)

Thus, we leveraged a “visual questioning-answering” model and fine tuned it to our specific requirements. In addition, the structured output format has allowed us to further processing the results and compensate for inaccuracies of the model.
